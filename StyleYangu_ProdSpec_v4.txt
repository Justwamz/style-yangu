1. Executive Summary
Style Yangu is a Kenyan-first fashion technology platform combining AI-powered personal styling, a seller showcase and commerce tool, and a tailor and artisan management system into one cohesive ecosystem. The name means "My Style" in Swahili, reflecting the platform's core promise: deeply personalised, honest fashion guidance rooted in local culture and identity.

� Consumers who waste time selecting outfits and cannot visualise how clothes look on their specific body type, skin tone, and colouring.
� Boutique sellers on Instagram and WhatsApp who cannot afford professional model shoots and manage their businesses through scattered messages and notebooks.
� Tailors and artisan makers including cobblers, bag makers, and jewellery makers who receive vague briefs, chase deposits, and have no digital presence.
� Enterprise retailers who need a structured channel to reach matched consumers without manual catalogue management.

Style Yangu launches as a PWA built as a monorepo: landing page, consumer app, seller app, tailor app, admin dashboard, and reseller portal. Every consumer has a named personal stylist � Amara (female) or Kofi (male) at MVP.

The platform operates a free-for-life consumer tier sustained by a local ad ecosystem. Sellers and artisans on paid plans can boost their items directly to matched free-tier consumers. This creates a self-funding community where consumer access costs nothing and seller ad spend covers the platform's cost of serving free users.

4.1.1 Camera-Only Policy for Wardrobe
All wardrobe item additions throughout the app � whether during onboarding or on an ongoing basis � require the user to photograph the item using the device camera. File uploads from the photo library are not permitted for wardrobe additions. This policy exists for three reasons:

� Integrity: the suggestion engine is designed around the user's actual wardrobe. Uploaded images from the internet corrupt the data and produce irrelevant suggestions.
� Anti-gaming: the daily wardrobe-addition unlock mechanic (photographing 2 new items to earn 1 extra suggestion) requires real physical items. Camera-only prevents consumers from downloading outfit photos online to farm suggestion unlocks.
� Quality: camera photos captured in the app go through the guided flat-lay overlay, lighting quality check, and compression pipeline. This produces consistent, well-tagged wardrobe data.

  Onboarding wardrobe capture is the only exception to the no-upload rule: screenshots from Instagram store posts may be submitted during onboarding to add items the user has bought or plans to buy. These screenshots are processed by AI for colour, category, and style tagging but are flagged as "purchased/planned" rather than "owned and available", so the suggestion engine treats them appropriately.

4.3.3 Unlock Mechanic: Wardrobe Camera Addition
The consumer can photograph 2 new clothing items using the camera (with the flat-lay overlay guide) to unlock 1 additional suggestion. This can be done twice per day to reach the 3-suggestion cap without watching any ads.

� Camera must be used � no uploads from photo library permitted
� Items photographed are added to the wardrobe catalogue and AI-tagged automatically
� The unlock is awarded immediately after the second photo is captured and processed
� Consumer can mix methods: 1 wardrobe addition pair and 1 ad to reach their cap
� The camera flat-lay overlay and lighting quality indicator apply as standard

  This mechanic reinforces the platform's core philosophy: Style Yangu is your personal stylist based on your actual clothes. Every wardrobe addition makes the suggestion engine more accurate and more personal. Rewarding additions with extra suggestions creates a virtuous loop that benefits the consumer, the AI, and the platform simultaneously.

4.3.5 Nudge Timing: Learned Behaviour
The daily first-suggestion nudge is not sent at a fixed time. The app learns from usage patterns: a consumer who consistently opens the app at 7am gets nudged at 7am. A consumer who opens it at 9am gets nudged at 9am. This learned timing makes the nudge feel personal and well-timed rather than mechanical. The pattern is established after 5 to 7 days of consistent usage and updates continuously.

7. Ad Boost Product
7.1 Overview
Ad Boost allows sellers and artisans on paid subscription tiers to place their items and services directly in front of matched free-tier consumers during the outfit suggestion flow. This is the mechanism that funds the free-for-life consumer tier.

Ad Boost is built at MVP launch but activated only when the consumer base reaches 1,000 to 2,000 active users. Until activation, all sellers and artisans see a "Coming Soon" treatment with a waitlist signup option. The infrastructure, matching logic, frequency caps, analytics, and pack purchase flow are all built and tested before a single real ad is served.

  Eligibility: Ad Boost is available to paid subscription tiers only. Free trial sellers and post-trial free sellers cannot purchase boost packs or access the ad dashboard. This prevents the ad product from becoming a subscription bypass.  
7.2 Coming Soon Treatment and Waitlist
Before activation, every paid seller and artisan sees an Ad Boost card in their dashboard showing:

� "Ad Boost � Coming Soon. Reach consumers who match your style profile while they browse outfit suggestions."

� Waitlist signup: "Join the waitlist to be first to know when we launch" � captures email and tier

Waitlist rollout order on activation: Brand sellers first, then Boutique, then Hustler. Master Artisan before Fundi. This rewards the highest-value subscribers with early access and creates a reason to be on a higher tier before the product is live.

The waitlist data also validates demand before activation. If 80% of Brand sellers join the waitlist and 20% of Hustler sellers do, that informs the activation timeline and pricing confidence.

11. Privacy and Content Safety
11.1 Guided Camera: Oval Face Overlay
Style Yangu never captures the user's face for storage or identity processing. The canvas-based oval overlay prevents face capture at source.

Implementation: camera preview rendered to a canvas element. Oval drawn over canvas at face height in real time. Gaussian blur applied to oval region via Canvas 2D API. When photo is captured, canvas frame with baked-in blur is saved. Original unblurred frame is never written to memory or storage.

Exception � selfie for avatar: oval inverted to guide face into centre of frame. Selfie is captured, processed by Imagen 3, original deleted immediately. Only cartoon output stored.

14.6 Ad Boost Service Architecture
The ads service (services/ads) manages the complete ad lifecycle independently of the main API for performance isolation.

ComponentResponsibilityInventory pool managerMaintains a real-time pool of available boost slots with their targeting criteria. Refreshes every 15 minutes. Feeds the matching engine.Matching engineGiven a consumer profile and a request for a sponsored card, selects the most relevant available boost slot using the priority stack (wishlist match > profile match > category affinity). Uses Claude Haiku for profile comparison.Frequency cap enforcerChecks Redis cache before serving any sponsored card. Rejects if seller has already appeared to this consumer today (1x cap) or 3x this week. Updates cache on every impression.Phase controllerReads the current ad frequency phase from admin config. Returns the number of sponsored cards required to unlock one organic suggestion. Responds to admin phase changes immediately.Slot trackerRecords every impression against the seller's weekly slot allocation. Triggers low slots alert at 80% usage. Resets all allocations Monday midnight Nairobi time.Analytics aggregatorCollects impression, wishlist save, follow, and Talk to Seller tap events. Aggregates by seller, item, time period, and consumer segment. Writes to boost campaign records in Render PostgreSQL.Archetype render managerAt boost slot activation, checks R2 for existing archetype renders per item. Generates missing renders via Imagen 3 in a background job (~40 renders per item). Blocks the boost slot from going live until the full archetype set is complete. On showcase image change, invalidates and re-renders affected archetypes only. Stores renders at key sponsored_card/{item_id}/{archetype_id} in Cloudflare R2.Coming soon gateWhen ad boost is in coming soon mode, this component intercepts all ad requests and returns a coming soon response.
